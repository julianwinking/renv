ALCE: Automatic LLM Citation Evaluation

Large language models that answer questions should support their statements with
citations so that readers can verify them. We introduce ALCE, the first benchmark
for automatic evaluation of citations generated by large language models.

ALCE evaluates citation quality along two axes. Citation recall measures whether
the set of cited passages together entail the generated statement. Citation
precision flags any individual cited passage that is irrelevant and does not
support the statement, using a natural language inference model.

Unlike commercial search assistants that cite an entire web page, ALCE cites at
the granularity of short hundred-word passages, because smaller passages are
easier for a human to verify against the claim.
