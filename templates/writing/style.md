# Reusable research sentences and argument constructions

Patterns worth reusing when drafting. Fill the brackets; delete the scaffold.
Add your own good sentences here whenever you write one worth keeping.

## Positioning (related work)
- "While [prior work] addresses [X] under [assumption], it leaves [Y] open;
  we target exactly this case."
- "[Method A] and [Method B] share [mechanism]; they differ in [axis], which
  matters because [consequence]."
- "We adopt [component] from [work] unchanged, so our contribution is
  isolated to [delta]."

## Claim → evidence → implication (the results paragraph)
- "[Finding stated plainly]. Across [setup], [metric] [changes] from [a] to
  [b] ([conditions]). This supports [claim], because [mechanism]."
- "The effect survives [control/ablation], ruling out [alternative
  explanation]."
- "At [boundary condition] the effect vanishes, consistent with [theory] —
  evidence that the mechanism is [X] rather than [Y]."

## Honest hedging (calibrated, not mushy)
- "We demonstrate [X] in [scope]; whether it extends to [broader scope]
  remains open (Section [limitations])."
- "This is provenance, not proof: [what is guaranteed] — not [what is not]."
- "We observe, but cannot yet explain, [anomaly]; we record it rather than
  smooth it over."

## Argument constructions
- **Calibration first:** "Before measuring [effect], we verify the harness
  reproduces [known result] (predicted [x], measured [y])."
- **Budget-matched comparison:** "Both conditions receive identical [budget];
  any difference is attributable to [the manipulated axis] alone."
- **Steelman then answer:** "The strongest objection is [objection]. If true,
  we would expect [signature]; we observe [counter-evidence] instead."

## Words to avoid
"clearly", "obviously", "significant" (without a test), "novel" (show the
delta instead), "very". Prefer verbs over nominalizations: "we measure", not
"measurement was performed".
