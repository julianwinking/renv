The Noisy Path from Source to Citation

When a paper cites a prior work, the citing sentence does not always faithfully
represent what the original source actually claimed. We study this loss of
fidelity at scale across roughly thirteen million citation sentence pairs.

We find a telephone effect: when an intermediary paper cites an original source
with low fidelity, downstream papers that cite the intermediary inherit and
propagate that distortion, drifting further from the original claim with each hop.

We caution that our quasi-experimental design does not guarantee that the
intermediary paper was actually read, and the measured effect size on our
five-point fidelity scale is small.
